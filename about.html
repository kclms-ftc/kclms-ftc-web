About Volcanix

Our story, our values and the plan for the team.

From Maths to Mechanisms

KCLMS Volcanix is the robotics team at King's College London Mathematics School. A few of us had done FTC before; the rest learned on the job. We started the team to do something hands-on with the maths and engineering we study. We won the Think Award in our first season, and we want the team to outlast us — each year's members pass the robot, and what they worked out, to the next group.

Meet the Team

Open Source by Principle

We publish our code and write up how we built things. FTC is small enough that teams get further by sharing, and a lot of what we know came from other teams doing the same.

Our Core Values

What drives the team

01

Innovation

We try unusual designs and drop them when testing points to something better.

02

Collaboration

Mechanical, programming, outreach and finance all feed into the same robot, so we work closely across the team.

03

Excellence

After each build and each competition we go through what went wrong and fix it for next time.

04

Accessibility

You do not need experience to join, just an interest in building things.

05

Community

We run school visits and help other rookie teams get set up.

06

Learning

Most of what we know came from things that did not work the first time.

Why FTC at KCLMS

Where equations meet mechanisms

01

Theory to Practice

KCLMS teaches pure mathematics; FTC is where we apply it to engineering problems with deadlines.

02

University-Ready Skills

Programming, CAD, project management and technical communication.

03

International Stage

We represent KCLMS against teams from other countries.

04

Lasting Connections

The competitions put us in the same room as students, mentors and engineers we would not meet otherwise.

Beyond This Season

We want the team to keep going after we leave, with each year's members training the next group and handing over the robot.
