## üìê PHOTOGRAPHY GUIDELINES

### Technical Settings:
- **Resolution**: Minimum 1920x1080, prefer 4K
- **Format**: JPG for web, keep RAW files for archives
- **Lighting**: Natural light or well-lit indoor spaces, avoid harsh shadows
- **Focus**: Sharp focus on main subject, slight blur OK for action shots

### Composition Tips:
- **Team photos**: Everyone visible, no one obscured, robot prominent
- **Robot photos**: Clean background (black, white, or yellow to match brand)
- **Action shots**: Capture genuine moments, avoid posed/stiff expressions
- **Close-ups**: Show detail of mechanisms, code on screens, hands at work
- **Wide shots**: Establish context (workshop, competition venue, etc.)

### Content Guidelines:
- Always ask permission before posting photos with faces
- Get photo release forms signed by all team members
- Avoid capturing other teams' proprietary designs at competitions
- School uniform/appropriate attire in all photos
- Ensure workspace looks organized (clean up before photos)

### Photo Organization:
```
/photos
```

## üìù WRITING TONE GUIDELINES

- **Professional but approachable**: Not overly formal, show personality
- **Student-led**: Emphasize it's BY students FOR students
- **Technical but accessible**: Explain jargon, welcome newcomers
- **Achievement-focused**: Highlight successes without arrogance
- **Inclusive**: "No experience required" message throughout
- **Action-oriented**: Use active voice, strong verbs
- **Passionate**: Let enthusiasm for robotics shine through

**Example Good Tone:**
"We're not just building a robot - we're building a team. Every line of code, every mechanism we design, every strategy we develop brings us closer to our goal: competing at the highest level while inspiring the next generation of engineers."

**Example Avoid:**
"FTC KCLMS is a competitive robotics organization that participates in sanctioned FIRST Tech Challenge events pursuant to international guidelines."
